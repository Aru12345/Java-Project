History  - Origins in China: While matcha is strongly associated with Japan, its origins can be traced back to China during the Tang Dynasty (618-907 CE). Chinese monks introduced the cultivation and consumption of powdered tea to Japan around the 9th century.Matcha became an integral part of Japanese culture, especially within Zen Buddhism. The Japanese tea ceremony, known as "chanoyu" or "sado," emerged as a ritualistic practice centered around the preparation, serving, and consumption of matcha. This ceremony emphasized principles of harmony, respect, purity, and tranquility.
